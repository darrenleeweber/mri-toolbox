Overview

As described in the introduction, coronal slices are excellent for identifying the hippocampus (you can see the folded shape of the hippocampus body, e.g. see the -8mm slices). Some landmarks to look for are:

  • amygdala (burgundy)
  • caudate nucleus (magenta)
  • claustrum (deep blue) - easier to see in axial views
  • globus pallidus (green)
  • hippocampus (brown)
  • putamen (yellow)
  • nucleus accumbens (orange)
  • thalamus (red) - poorly defined in T1 scans
  • parietal operculum (dotted green)
  • temporal operculum (dotted pink)
  • insular lobe (dotted light blue)

Chris Rorden, 13 October 2002
